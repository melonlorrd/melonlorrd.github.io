# There Is No Such Thing as Random or Luck

Hello there. Welp, I did promise I would eventually show off some cool stuff I built, and I swear I have been trying out a lot of things lately, but most of them are still stuck in that awkward phase where they are either half-finished, badly optimized, or simply not good enough yet for me to proudly put them in front of people and go “look what I made,” and on top of that work has been absolutely hectic lately, so my brain has basically been operating in survival mode for weeks now.

Anyways, I wanted to throw this idea out into the wild today because it has been sitting in my head for a while now.

I genuinely do not think there is such a thing as luck or randomness, at least not in the way people emotionally interpret those things, because what most people call “luck” is usually just an outcome born from an absurd amount of interconnected events, decisions, timing, circumstances, people, systems, emotions, and reactions colliding together in ways too complicated for any single person to fully understand or predict, and because we cannot map all of it properly, we simply label it random to make peace with our lack of visibility.

When I was younger, I knew someone from my childhood who was honestly brilliant, like genuinely brilliant, she practically taught me things growing up and she was one of those people who clearly had the intelligence and capability to go extremely far in life, but somewhere along the way she got deeply into reiki, manifestation, attraction, spiritual energy and all those ideas where people believe they can somehow align reality with desire if they think positively enough or “ask the universe” correctly enough.

Now look, maybe she is happy, and honestly if she is then good for her, because peace matters more than achievement in a lot of ways, but a part of me still feels she could have gone so much further if she had simply accepted that there are things in this world that are fundamentally not your choice to make, and no amount of belief, manifestation, visualization, prayer, or positive energy changes that reality.

The best thing you can realistically do is make sure that the choices which are under your control are not stupid ones, that when life eventually throws an opportunity in your direction you are at least prepared enough to not destroy it with your own incompetence or self-sabotage.

And honestly, accepting that hurts, because human beings desperately want control over outcomes, we want to believe effort guarantees reward, we want to believe suffering eventually gets compensated, and we especially want to believe the universe somehow keeps score and rewards “good energy,” but I do not think reality works that way at all.

Later in life, I met almost the opposite type of person, a manager who was also deeply spiritual and completely convinced he manifested his success, his promotions, his achievements and his lifestyle through mindset and energy and whatever else, and maybe he does have a good life externally, maybe he has a loving wife, maybe he has a healthy family, maybe from the outside he looks like someone who “figured life out,” but he was also one of the worst people I have ever worked under because he bullied juniors constantly, stole credit from people below him, manipulated conversations to make himself look smarter, and overall created an environment where everyone beneath him felt smaller.

And I think that is another side of these beliefs that bothers me, because sometimes when people start attributing everything to supernatural systems or destiny or manifestation, they stop developing genuine self-awareness, because if every success is proof the universe supports you, then eventually you start believing your own behavior no longer needs criticism.

Personally, I do not need fear of hell to stop me from harassing people, and I do not need promises of heaven to help someone who genuinely needs help, because I do not really think morality becomes more meaningful when tied to cosmic rewards or punishments.

And maybe that sounds cold depending on who reads this, but strangely I think accepting this view of life actually makes existence more meaningful, not less, because once you stop believing there is some grand invisible force secretly arranging your destiny, you start appreciating the reality that this is probably the only life you get, which means your choices, your character, your effort, your failures, and the way you treat people become infinitely more important precisely because there is no guaranteed cosmic correction waiting later.

Maybe you achieve your dreams, maybe you do not, maybe your potential never fully gets recognized, maybe your path becomes something completely different from what you imagined originally or maybe your talent is weird enough that you accidentally create your own blend of genres.

But what people call randomness is usually just complexity beyond comprehension, millions of people making decisions independently without knowing you even exist, systems reacting to systems, events triggering other events, timing colliding with circumstance, and eventually all of that produces an outcome that emotionally feels random from your limited perspective. Uhh like, shit happens or what's happened, happened.
